Halloween (shortened to "All Hallows' Eve") is a traditional festival celebrated on October 31 every year, on the evening before All Saints' Day in Western Christianity. This is the beginning of All Saints' Day – the time of the liturgical year dedicated to remembering the dead, including saints, martyrs and all faithful believers who have died. Halloween's traditional focus revolves around the theme of using "humor and ridicule to confront the power of death."
